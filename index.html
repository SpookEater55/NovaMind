My Journey

In 2007, at just 7 years old, I discovered the magic of coding. While most kids my age were playing outside, I was in front of an old family computer teaching myself HTML, CSS, and basic JavaScript. That early spark never faded.

Over the next 19 years, I immersed myself deeply into technology. I built countless projects, learned many programming languages, and constantly challenged myself to create better tools and systems.

One of the most exciting milestones was becoming an early developer on Picoin, where I gained real-world experience working on a live crypto project during its formative days.

The idea for NovaMind was born from a frustrating personal experience. After a long trip, I arrived at a hotel exhausted, only to wait nearly 45 minutes at the front desk because the staff was overwhelmed. As I stood there tired and impatient, I thought: “There has to be a better way.”

That moment became the catalyst. I decided to use everything I had learned over nearly two decades of coding to build an intelligent AI assistant that could instantly handle guest communications, reservations, welcome messages, and routine tasks — dramatically reducing wait times and improving service for everyone.

From a 7-year-old kid coding in 2007 to building AI tools at 26 — the journey continues.
